# Swan's Office --- Interactive Portfolio Concept

## Vision

Instead of a traditional scrolling portfolio, visitors enter **Swan's
Office**---a single cozy office scene where a cool duck (a playful nod
to *Sowan* → *Swan*) acts as the host.

This is **not a game** in the traditional sense. It is a **living
interactive illustration**.

The office is alive: - Coffee gently steams. - Pipe smoke drifts
upward. - The duck blinks, reads a newspaper, yawns or naps. - Rain
falls outside the window. - The clock ticks. - Small environmental
animations make the room feel inhabited.

The visitor doesn't navigate through pages. They interact with the duck.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Core Philosophy

The duck is never a decoration.

The duck is the **host**.

Every section of the portfolio is introduced by the duck before it
appears.

Nothing opens instantly.

Every interaction feels like talking to someone in their office.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Reference Scene

The original inspiration is a cozy, slightly messy office.

Imagine:

-   A duck in a business suit sitting comfortably behind a large wooden
    desk.
-   Reading a newspaper with a smoking pipe.
-   Coffee steaming beside him.
-   Sticky notes.
-   Trophy shelf.
-   Filing cabinets.
-   Bookshelves.
-   Whiteboard.
-   Plants.
-   Clock.
-   Window with city skyline and rain.
-   Warm lighting.

Everything feels lived in.

Nothing feels sterile.

The office tells a story before a single word is spoken.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Camera

The camera NEVER moves.

The viewer is standing in front of the desk as if visiting someone's
office.

The duck remains the center of attention.

This is intentionally easier to build than a top-down game while
creating a stronger emotional connection.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Interaction Flow

Visitor clicks an object.

Duck notices.

Duck looks directly at the viewer.

Dialogue appears.

Duck reacts.

Relevant portfolio content opens.

When closed...

Duck calmly returns to whatever he was doing.

This continuity creates the illusion of life.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Example Interaction

Visitor clicks Projects.

Duck lowers the newspaper.

Looks directly at the visitor.

"Oh... curious about my projects?"

"Fair enough."

"Let me show you."

The monitor lights up (or a large storybook opens on the desk).

Projects appear.

Closing the window:

The book closes.

Duck puts it away.

Returns to reading.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Duck Personality

The duck should feel like a real office worker.

Idle: - Reading newspaper - Sleeping - Drinking coffee - Looking
outside - Blinking - Stretching

When interrupted: - Looks at viewer - Smiles - Thinks - Speaks naturally

The duck is confident, humorous and welcoming.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Dialogue Style

Instead of robotic answers:

Projects: "Projects, huh?"

"Trying to see if I actually know what I'm doing?"

"Fair enough."

Experience: "Let's dig through the paperwork..."

Resume: "Freshly updated."

Awards: "Still chasing gold."

These small jokes create personality.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Visual Language

Warm colors.

Comfortable office.

Messy but organized.

Everything should suggest that someone genuinely works here.

Think of the atmosphere rather than realism.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Visual Creation Plan

We are NOT drawing everything manually.

AI will help produce assets.

Process:

1.  Establish one consistent art direction.
2.  Create concept art.
3.  Generate matching assets.
4.  Edit them until they all belong in the same world.
5.  Animate only important elements.

Assets include:

-   Desk
-   Chair
-   Coffee
-   Duck
-   Newspaper
-   Pipe
-   Books
-   Trophy
-   Computer
-   Window
-   Lamp
-   Whiteboard
-   Plants
-   Filing cabinets
-   Dialogue box
-   Book UI
-   Folder UI

The biggest goal is visual consistency.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Animation Plan

Most objects remain static.

Small looping animations create life.

Examples:

-   Steam
-   Smoke
-   Blink
-   Clock
-   Rain
-   Clouds
-   Plant sway
-   Light glow

The duck has separate animations:

-   Idle
-   Reading
-   Sleeping
-   Looking at viewer
-   Talking
-   Thinking
-   Smiling
-   Returning to idle

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Storybook UI

Instead of browser popups:

Projects open as a book.

Experience opens as a folder.

Resume appears as a physical document.

Everything feels like it belongs on the desk.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# AI

The duck can eventually answer natural language questions.

It should know:

-   Resume
-   Projects
-   Skills
-   Experience
-   GitHub
-   LinkedIn

The AI is the duck---not a floating chatbot.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Mobile Responsiveness

This project must be designed mobile-first.

Requirements:

-   Entire office scales proportionally.
-   Tap replaces hover.
-   Objects remain large enough to tap comfortably.
-   Dialogue occupies only part of the screen.
-   Book UI adapts into a readable mobile layout.
-   Maintain animations without hurting performance.
-   Keep loading fast.

Desktop should reveal more detail, but mobile must remain fully usable.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
